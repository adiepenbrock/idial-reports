The newly established transfer lab creates a physical and digital infrastructure where researchers, public administrators, and business representatives can collaborate on practice-oriented prototypes. In its first half-year of operation, three co-design cycles were conducted with a total of 42 external partners. The lab features dedicated workspaces for data visualization, simulation, and user-centered design. It is conceived as an open platform providing low-threshold access to research expertise and systematically accelerating knowledge transfer into municipal practice.

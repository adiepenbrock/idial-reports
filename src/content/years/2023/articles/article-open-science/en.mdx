Reproducibility is not an optional property of scientific results but an infrastructure mission requiring institutional investment and coordinated standards. This article describes how the institute introduced a shared toolchain for data versioning, containerization, and automated testing in 2023 that measurably raised reproducibility rates from 61 to 89 percent. The decisive factor was the combination of technical tools with binding editorial processes jointly developed and adapted by all research groups. The insights feed into a pan-European position paper on Open Science standards. The framework is published under a CC-BY licence and available for adoption by other research institutions.

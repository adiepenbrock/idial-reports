Knowledge graphs provide a powerful method for integrating heterogeneous data sources into a shared semantic model. This article presents a transdisciplinary framework that merges geospatial data, social indicators, and policy scenarios into a unified graph capable of directly informing urban planning processes. Application to three major German cities demonstrates that the approach predicts heat island risks with an accuracy exceeding 87 percent. The graph structure simultaneously enables interactive exploration of interactions between infrastructure, demographics, and climate variables. The method is documented as an open framework and can be adopted directly by other research groups.

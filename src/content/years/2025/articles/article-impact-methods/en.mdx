Classical performance indicators such as publication counts or third-party funding volumes capture the societal impact of applied research only incompletely. This article proposes a multidimensional metrics system that combines quantitative indicators with qualitative impact narratives. Core elements include a transfer maturity index, evidence of uptake in practice organisations, and structured stakeholder retrospectives. The system was co-developed with 23 research institutions and tested in a one-year pilot program. Results show greater acceptance among funders and better manageability of transfer processes compared with purely quantitative approaches.

Cooperative research alliances face a structural tension between the scientific autonomy of individual institutions and the coordination requirements of shared infrastructures and goals. This article develops an operating model for network governance based on four principles: subsidiary decision structures, standardised interfaces rather than central control, explicit trust agreements between partners, and shared metric frameworks for impact measurement. The model was tested in a European research alliance with 14 institutions over three years and demonstrates that stable governance significantly increases collaboration depth and publication output. A reference implementation of the governance documentation is available as an open template repository.

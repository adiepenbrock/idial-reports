Dense urban regions present particular challenges for energy supply: load peaks arise in a spatially concentrated, short-term manner under difficult-to-predict conditions. This article presents a machine learning model that combines historical consumption, weather, and mobility data to forecast load peaks with a mean prediction error below four percent. The method was piloted in cooperation with three municipal grid operators and reduced load management response times by 38 percent. The model is available as open-source software and is actively developed by the research community. Results demonstrate that data-driven approaches significantly outperform classical rule-based systems during extreme load events.

Secure and legally compliant sharing of research data across institutional boundaries remains one of the central open questions in data science. This article develops a governance pattern that combines federated access rights, privacy-preserving analysis techniques, and standardised consent structures in a unified framework. The pattern was tested with eight European research institutions in the health data domain, enabling legally sound analysis of privacy-sensitive datasets across three jurisdictions for the first time. Acceptance by data protection authorities and ethics committees was consistently high. A reference implementation is available as an open-source library.
